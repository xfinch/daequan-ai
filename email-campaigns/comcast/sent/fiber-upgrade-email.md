## Why 50/50 Fiber Beats 800/300 Coax

**1. Symmetrical Speeds (The Upload Problem)**

Coax gives you asymmetrical speeds — fast downloads, slow uploads. According to FatBeam Fiber, "symmetrical internet provides equal upload and download speeds... eliminating those frustrating bottlenecks when trying to share important work." [^1]

With fiber, 50 Mbps up means 50 Mbps up. With coax, that "300 Mbps upload" is theoretical — and shared.

**2. Real-World Performance (The "Up To" Problem)**

Wolontek's 2025 analysis found that "fiber connections stay consistent even when streaming 4K video, video conferencing, gaming, and large file transfers simultaneously, whereas cable lines often slow down during peak usage." [^2]

Your 800 Mbps? That's "up to" 800 Mbps. During business hours when everyone's online? You're competing with the coffee shop next door for bandwidth.

**3. Dedicated vs. Shared (The Neighborhood Problem)**

Coax is a shared pipe. Fiber is a dedicated line to your building. As Cabling Solutions Group notes, "If you're hosting video calls or running cloud-based applications, this increased latency could result in noticeable lag. Fiber is not only faster but also more reliable." [^3]
