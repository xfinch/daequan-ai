### **🚀 THIS MONTH: The Truth About Speed (And Why Bigger Isn't Always Better)**

**"I have 500 Mbps internet. Why would I need your 50 Mbps fiber?"**

We hear this question a lot. And honestly? It's a fair question — until you understand what those numbers actually mean.

**The Real Story: Download vs. Upload**

That "500 Mbps" connection? It's probably 500 Mbps **down** and 35 Mbps **up**.

Here's the thing: business doesn't happen in one direction anymore. You're not just *consuming* the internet — you're *producing* on it.

**When does upload speed matter?**
- Cloud backups running overnight (or all day)
- Video calls with clients, vendors, remote teams
- Point-of-sale systems syncing to the cloud
- Security cameras uploading footage
- File sharing with partners who need *your* documents
- VPN connections for remote workers accessing your servers

**The Comcast Difference: Dedicated Internet Access (DIA)**

With our fiber-based Dedicated Internet, that 50/50 means **50 Mbps down AND 50 Mbps up** — symmetric, guaranteed, and uncontested.

Think of it like this:
- **Cable/Coax (HFC)** = A wide highway with one narrow on-ramp. Great for streaming Netflix. Terrible when your whole team is trying to push data upstream.
- **Dedicated Internet (Fiber)** = A two-way street with guaranteed lanes in both directions. What you pay for is what you get — period.

**Why "Contended" Matters**

Cable internet is "contended" — you're sharing that 500 Mbps with your neighbors. At 9 AM on a Tuesday? Fine. At 2 PM when three businesses on your block are running backups and video calls? You're fighting for bandwidth.

Dedicated Internet is **yours**. No sharing. No slowdowns during peak hours. No "well, it worked fine yesterday."

**TECH TALK: Understanding Your Internet Speed Test**

If you're curious about what you're actually getting from your current provider, run a speed test at fast.com or speedtest.net.

**Look at BOTH numbers.** If your download is 300 Mbps but your upload is 10 Mbps, you're going to hit a wall the moment your team tries to:
- Upload large files
- Run video conferences
- Sync to cloud storage
- Use cloud-based POS or CRM systems

Symmetric speeds (same up and down) aren't just nice to have anymore — they're essential for modern business.
