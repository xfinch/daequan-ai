## Core Synopsis

The sales team is converging on a central hypothesis: a specific customer is a high-value, high-intent target who is uniquely receptive to an infrastructure upgrade, but our current service offering creates a critical product gap. The customer's request for higher-speed internet (500Mbps/1Gbps) cannot be met with the existing hybrid network, which maxes out at 300Mbps. This forces a pivot to a fiber build-out, introducing significant complexity in cost ($7k estimate), timeline (90-120 days), and permitting. While the customer appears aggressive and financially prepared—leveraging special funding from the property's previous owners—the success of this deal now hinges entirely on our ability to transparently manage expectations around the new timeline and construction requirements. We are betting that his clarity and readiness to invest will overcome the friction of a longer, more complex deployment.

## Strategic Decision: Serviceability & Proposal Pivot

### 1. The Initial Opportunity
*   **Customer Profile**: A rare, high-clarity customer who is ready to invest in upgrading his restaurant. He has a clear vision, is not resistant to timelines, and has secured special funding to improve the property.
*   **Initial Discussion**: The conversation centered on the vibrant local area (Proctor neighborhood in Tacoma), the farmers market, and other local businesses, successfully building rapport and establishing the customer's commitment to the location.
*   **Identified Need**: The customer requires high-speed internet, specifically asking for price points on 500Mbps and 1Gbps tiers.

### 2. The Serviceability Gap
*   **The Blocker**: The current on-site "hybrid" network technology is limited to a maximum of 300Mbps. It cannot fulfill the customer's core requirement.
*   **The Required Solution**: Meeting the 500Mbps/1Gbps demand necessitates a new fiber installation.
*   **The Complication**: The fiber build-out introduces new variables:
    *   **Cost**: An initial desktop survey estimates a ~$7,000 construction cost. This is an estimate and requires a formal survey for a precise figure.
    *   **Timeline**: The project timeline extends from a standard installation to an estimated 90-120 days, largely dependent on city permitting.

### 3. The Path Forward
*   **The Verdict**: The team must pivot the proposal from a standard service sale to a small-scale construction project.
*   **Execution Plan**: The proposal will present two options:
    1.  The available 200/300Mbps hybrid plans.
    2.  The desired 500Mbps/1Gbps fiber plans, explicitly stating the associated construction costs and the extended, permit-dependent timeline.
*   **Strategic Rationale**: Given the customer's disposition and clear intent, a transparent, two-option proposal is deemed the most efficient path. It puts the decision in his hands while managing expectations about the significant difference in deployment complexity between the two tiers of service.
